[ENTRY 1 ] Book Design Covers — What attracted me overall when researching through covers online/irl is either non-traditional formats and good imagery. I shamefully do judge a book by its cover. Book 1 is printed on fabric and I think that just brings a more engaging look through and physical satisfaction. The pages all beautifully blend into one another, and have this bold edge. The cover attracted me for the "sacredness" it gives but also how welcoming the fabric makes it. Book 2 is this kinda rotten cover insert with the inside sliding it out. I like this format of allowing the reader to fully engage with uncovering the book and immersing themselves in it. The inside unfolds like an accordion, and also has nice translucent pages inside. Book 3 is a religious pamphlet with a quiet alluring image and printed on good low-quality paper. The pages felt silky and plastic, but I enjoyed it almost like receipt paper. The image on the cover is what drew me apart from the absurd context, I think it is a satisfying graphic.
